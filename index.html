Introduction

Sir Tim Berners-Lee (born 1955) is a British computer scientist and the inventor of the World Wide Web. I admire him for his visionary innovation, humility, and dedication to making information accessible to all.

Achievements

In 1989, while working at CERN, Tim Berners-Lee proposed a system that became the World Wide Web, combining hypertext with the internet. He wrote the first web browser and server, launching the web in 1991. His decision to make the web open and free has transformed how we communicate, work, and learn.
